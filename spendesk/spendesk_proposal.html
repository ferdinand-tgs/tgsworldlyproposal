How we’d approach your case

Three things you raised. Here is where we stand.

Take over, act in-house

We build on what works. A clean handover with Loretta, a fresh-eyes audit, then we run it as an extension of your team. Everyone is senior, so nothing stalls when your team is busy.

Two motions, one goal: volume

Kept separate, run in parallel. Google Search and PMax carry the high-intent prospecting motion; LinkedIn ABM carries the mid-market accounts. Separate budgets and signals let us push volume without the two degrading each other.

Pipeline you can predict

Our process

Seven steps, from strategic alignment to a multi-channel programme.

The people who audit the accounts are the people who run them. Here is the sequence we’d follow with Spendesk.

01Strategic alignment

Company, goals, and commercial strategy; the ICP and the segments that matter; how sales defines a good lead. This is where paid connects to pipeline and to how sales actually sells. Led by Laura.

02Paid audit + fixes

A full external read of your Google and LinkedIn accounts and tracking, plus a clean handover from Loretta. Quick wins (exclusions, negative keywords, tracking gaps, weak creative) go live in parallel, without waiting for the full audit.

03Campaign design & setup

Rebuild or refine campaign structure by motion (prospecting vs. mid-market / ABM) and by market, with native-language creative and landing pages.

04Continuous optimization

Weekly optimization against pipeline and cost targets: bids, budgets, audiences, creative rotation, and negative-keyword mining, market by market.

05Targeted experiments

Structured tests across new audiences, messaging angles, landing pages, and channels (Meta, Bing, Reddit), run as controlled experiments so we learn fast without risking the core programme.

06Multi-channel campaign

Coordinated campaigns across Google and LinkedIn, run in step with your internal team, sales, and the SEO agency already in place, so paid reinforces the wider motion instead of competing with it.

07Pull in the wider cast · optional

When a specific initiative needs unblocking, whether design, content, landing-page development, or data architecture and AI workflows, we bring in Daniela, Stefana, Alex, or Diego on demand, without a standing retainer for skills you don’t need every month.

The steps feed each other: what we learn optimizing one market and motion informs the next, and the tracking layer keeps pipeline and cost honest across all of it. We’re accountable to pipeline and the efficiency of the spend behind it.

Bottom-up: is the channel working?

  • Reaching the right people. The right ICP, accounts, and seniority.
  • Getting the right engagement. Quality signals like qualified clicks and form quality.
  • Maximizing return on ad spend. Bids, budgets, audiences, and creative optimized at the campaign level, market by market.

Top-down: is it driving pipeline?

  • Pipeline influenced and sourced by paid, tied back to your commercial goals.
  • Cost per SQL and per opportunity, tracked as the primary efficiency metric.
  • Multi-touch attribution blended with self-reported data. That’s the honest way to handle the “paid up, direct up” effect you’re seeing.
Where this goes next: signal stacking. At Spendesk’s scale there is enough data to move from attribution to prediction. Over time we build predictive models that estimate the pipeline return of a given investment before you commit it, supported by Diego, our data and RevOps engineer. It isn’t day-one work. It’s where a programme this size should head over time.
